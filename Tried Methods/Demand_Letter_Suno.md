## I. FACTUAL BASIS

### A. Unauthorized Proof-of-Work Computation

Your platform deploys hCaptcha's proof-of-work system on every page load. This system executes cryptocurrency-style computational challenges on users' CPU hardware without disclosure. Our forensic analysis documented blockchain references in hCaptcha's network response payloads. This computational work:

- Was never disclosed in your Terms of Service or Privacy Policy
- Generated economic value for hCaptcha's network at the user's expense
- Consumed electricity and degraded CPU hardware
- Constitutes unauthorized use of computer resources under the South Carolina Computer Crime Act (§ 16-16-20) and the federal Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (18 U.S.C. § 1030)

### B. GPU Compositor Abuse

Your deployment of Microsoft Clarity session replay, combined with your platform's continuous UI re-rendering during music generation, created a GPU compositor feedback loop that caused:

- Visible "window warping" — rendering artifacts visible across all application windows
- Frame dropping and stuttering on standard consumer hardware
- Extended GPU thermal stress contributing to component degradation

### C. Browser Profile Bloat

Your tracker stack's accumulated data caused browser profile sizes to reach 9.6 GB, degrading overall system performance and requiring manual remediation.

### D. Undisclosed Privacy Violations

Your platform loads 71+ third-party tracking scripts without adequate disclosure, including session replay technology that records all user interactions without meaningful informed consent.

### E. Deceptive Privacy Policy Update

On approximately February 12, 2026, you added "Interactive Chat Information" as a data collection category — using creative inputs for model training — without adequate notice, published terms, or opt-in consent.
